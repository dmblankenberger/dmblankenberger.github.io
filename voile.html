Voile

Investigating the potential of steel

The careful entanglement of steel serves as a delicate, poetic material rather than its tough, forbearing counterpart. Beginning with lightweight steel as a building material, the skin also functions as the structure, thus creating a more inviting interior-exterior relationship. Steel molds and meshes to form a delicate entanglement of pieces, reinforcing the experience within. Referencing iconic New Orleans wrought-iron balconies, the interwoven gridshell creates a dynamic lighting effect, casting shadows that entice people to indirectly experience the unique structure. The steel interacts at human level, creating interstitial spaces between gridshell and interior program.





Referencing the New Orleans distinctive ironwork balconies, the gridshell exhibits a quality of filtered light and interior-exterior relationships that influence human interactions. Likewise, the gridshell creates a dappled lighting effect that casts dynamic shadows. In this way, people indirectly experience the structural gridshell wherever they venture in the library. The sun shading system reinforces the dappled lighting effect. Planks run along the North-South axis, gradually converging and dispersing depending on programmatic needs, and further sheltering spaces that require protection from the harsh New Orleans sunlight.

The steel interacts at human level, creating interstitial spaces between gridshell and interior program. This hybrid interwoven skin-structure encourages interaction between the two sides of the site (facing the Lafitte Greenway and St. Louis Street) and also social interactions to blend these boundaries.

Schematic floor plans


Voile is located along the Lafitte Greenway in a location with high promise. Treme, New Orleans is a region with cultural significance. The Voile design connects the Lafitte Greenway to the proposed hotel to the west and high school to the east. The open ground level of the library allows for visitors to actively or inactively encounter the space as the move across the greenway.
