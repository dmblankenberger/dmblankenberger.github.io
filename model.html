3D Modeling + Printing

From concept to completion

As a test of my 3D modeling skills, I attempted to make an elephant from scratch using Rhino as my primary software. It started out with simple geometric forms, using an image for reference. The complexity of the experiment was increased exponentially once the geometric forms needed to be merged. I experimented with the Rhino plug-in T-Splines to blend the shapes and push them into the correct forms.



The most challenging part of this project was creating a smooth single form. It was simple to model the individual parts, but welding them together and smoothing creases in the form was quite the undertaking. A secondary software called Meshmixer was used to prepare the final form for 3D printing: capping the bottoms of the feet and top of the tusk. The resulting elephant was printed on a MakerBot Replicator 2 with blue PLA filament. The raft and supports were easily removed. The figurine stands on its own without other support. It is available to be downloaded and printed on any 3D printing device via Thingiverse.
